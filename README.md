# Marketing-Campaign-Performance-Analysis 📊

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing and visualizing digital marketing campaign performance using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn in Google Colab.
The objective of the project is to evaluate the effectiveness of different marketing campaigns by examining key performance metrics such as:
- Rate
- Acquisition Cost
- Return on Investment (ROI)
- Engagement Score
- Clicks & Impressions

The project also explores:
- Channel effectiveness
- Customer segmentation
- Geographic influence
- Time-based campaign trends

Through Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and data visualization, actionable insights and recommendations were generated to improve future marketing strategies.

## Technologies Used

## Key Insights 
- Lower acquisition cost campaigns generally produced higher ROI.
- Facebook showed the highest overall ROI contribution.
- Engagement score had very weak correlation with conversion rate.
- The 25–34 age demographic showed strong campaign response.
- English-language campaigns performed slightly better than other languages.
- Chicago and Dallas generated higher ROI compared to other locations.
- Engagement trends increased steadily over time.

## Strategic Recommendations 
- Prioritize cost-efficient campaigns over high-budget campaigns.
- Invest more in high-performing channels such as Facebook.
- Focus on conversion-driven KPIs rather than engagement alone.
- Improve localization and audience-specific targeting strategies.
- Optimize campaigns in regions with high acquisition costs.
- Continuously perform A/B testing for campaign optimization.
- Use data-driven decision-making for budget allocation
